Design Process

Domain Research

Interviews Observation

After choosing the domain of outdoor apps, we conducted three user interviews with hikers, campers, and outdoor enthusiasts. We also observed online outdoor communities, such as the Vancouver Hiking subreddit and the Outdoor Vancouver Slack channel. A common topic of conversation involved recommending and exchanging gear from peers.



Analysis of Existing Apps

We also reviewed existing outdoor apps and websites such as All Trails, Peaks Finder, Map My Hike, and more. Common features were related to navigation, trail recommendations, activity/fitness tracking, and image/GPS recognition (ex: identifying mountain peaks or constellations by taking an image).

Persona Development

Based on the interviews and observation, we developed two personas to represent the two types of users for our app.

Note: At this earlier stage, we were considering a mix of buying/renting before we decided to focus solely on rentals.

Style Guide

A style guide was also developed to determine the app’s visual elements. The overall look is clean and minimal in order to let the images of equipment listings stand out. Green and blue accents were chosen based on elements from nature.

This style guide was applied to our mockups and later, an interactive prototype.
